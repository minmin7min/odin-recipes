Ginger Pork

Description

Ginger Pork, also known as Shogayaki, is a popular Japanese dish made with thinly sliced pork cooked in a sweet and savory ginger sauce.

It is quick to prepare and is commonly served with steamed rice and shredded cabbage.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound thinly sliced pork loin
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger root

Steps

  1. Mix together soy sauce, sake, mirin, and ginger in a large bowl until combined.
  2. Add sliced pork; stir to coat. Cover the bowl and marinate for about 1 hour.
  3. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Sauté pork in hot oil until browned and cooked through; discard marinade.
    An instant-read thermometer inserted into pork should read at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).
